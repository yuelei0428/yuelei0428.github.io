I'm an undergrad double majoring in Computer Engineering (honors track) and Joint Mathematics-Economics @ UC San Diego. I'm interested in interactive and generative modeling, with their applications in computer vision and robotics. I hope to build agents that can perceive and interact with the physical world as we do. During my undergraduate study, I'm advised by Prof Xiaolong Wang on robotics and Prof Nuno Vasconcelos on computer vision. I have also interned at EPFL in beautiful Switzerland where I was advised by Prof Pascal Frossard. Now I'm interning in Prof Hanspeter Pfister's group at Harvard. Though not my major research focus, I have also worked with Weitang Liu on some projects related to LLMs, energy based models, and sampling algorithms. He convinced me to work on this problem that very few people are tackling, and it has turned out to be a fun and rewarding experience :D
